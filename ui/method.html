Introduction

PDB(Protein Data Bank) maintains the world's largest protein database. By analyzing the it, we found that amino acids pairs have a very strong tendency in spatial distribution. Following two figure show the tendency in a contour map.

Based on the research, we establised the Spherical Coordinate System and analyzed different kind of amino acid pairs. The coordinate system is shown in the following figure.

For amino acids in a protein, every two amino acids can be regard as a pair. For the evaluation of the entire structure, not all pairs have positive effect. In fact, pairings that are too far can have a negative impact on the evaluation. We propose a "cutoff" in that background. According to the different settings of cutoff, Nepre generates two branching algorithms, Nepre-R and Nepre-F.

Nepre-R uses the radius sum of amino acid pair as the cutoff. Distance lower than the cutoff will be regard as the effective pair. We use 30,000 high-resolution protein and get the statistal result of each kind amino acid. Then, gaussian function is used to fit the statistical data and use mean value as the radius. Following figure shows the radius distribution. Details can be found at Radius Value.

Nepre-F uses a more universal value as the cutoff. Experiment shows that setting cutoff to 6 angstrom can get the best native structure recognition. The pearson coefficient distribution are shown in the following figure(Using decoy set 3DRobot from zhanglab).

Summary

Expement results show Nepre performs well on many decoy sets. It is a efficient and stable scoring function for protein structure assessment. Following figure shows a example of recognition the native structure on 1BYIA.
